# urban-cooling-spitalfields

Urban Heat Mitigation: Spatially Optimizing Tree Canopy in Spitalfields
​Data-Driven Advocacy for Green Infrastructure

​## Project Overview

​This project provides a data-driven framework for expanding the urban tree canopy on Toynbee Street, London (E1). By analyzing street capacity, species-specific growth patterns, and heat-island temperature profiles, this analysis supports a formal petition to the Tower Hamlets Council to fund 18 new tree pits using Section 106 and NCIL developer levies.

## The Problem: Urban Heat Island (UHI)
​Spitalfields is a high-density urban environment with minimal green cover. During summer peaks, Toynbee Street suffers from localized "heat traps" due to thermal mass in pavement and buildings.
​Current State: < 10% Canopy cover in the immediate ward.
​Target: 25% Canopy cover (Greater London Authority baseline).
​Risk: Heat-related health complications for pedestrians and residents.
## The Solution: Strategic Reforestation
​I modeled the implementation of Acer platanoides ‘Crimson Sentry’ (Columnar Norway Maple) based on its unique spatial footprint:
​Narrow Profile: Ideal for E1’s narrow pavements (prevents signage obstruction).
​Spacing Optimization: Calculated a 5-meter interval to balance root health with maximum shade contiguity.
​Projected Impact: Empirical modeling shows a potential 5°C reduction in ambient surface temperatures during peak 14:00–16:00 intervals.
##​ Data Insights
​Spatial Capacity: Through an audit of underground utilities and building entrances, I identified a realistic capacity for 18 additional trees.
